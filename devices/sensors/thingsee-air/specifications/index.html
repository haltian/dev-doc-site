Measurement Specifications

CO₂ (Carbon Dioxide) Sensor

SpecificationValue
TechnologyNDIR (Non-Dispersive Infrared)
Measurement Range400 - 5,000 ppm
Accuracy±30 ppm or ±3% of reading (whichever is greater)
Resolution1 ppm
Response Time (t63)< 60 seconds
Warm-up Time< 60 seconds
Long-term Drift< 3% over 15 years
Calibration MethodAutomatic baseline correction (ABC)
Calibration Interval8 days continuous
Baseline Target410 ppm (outdoor air)

Performance Notes:

  • Optimal accuracy requires periodic exposure to fresh air (410 ppm)
  • Best suited for environments with regular unoccupied periods
  • Automatic baseline correction compensates for sensor aging

TVOC (Total Volatile Organic Compounds) Sensor

SpecificationValue
TechnologyMetal oxide (MOX) semiconductor
Measurement Range0 - 18,000 ppb
SensitivityBroad spectrum VOC detection
Warm-up TimeInitial 48 hours for baseline
Baseline CalibrationAutomatic, continuous
Response TimeSeconds to minutes (compound dependent)

Detection Capability:

  • Alcohols, aldehydes, ketones
  • Aliphatic and aromatic hydrocarbons
  • Amines, organic acids
  • Cooking odors, cleaning products
  • Building material off-gassing

Limitations:

  • Provides total VOC index, not individual compound identification
  • Sensitivity varies by compound type
  • Not suitable for regulatory compliance measurements requiring specific VOC identification

Environmental Limits

Do not expose to:

  • Direct sunlight for prolonged periods
  • Condensing humidity or water spray
  • Corrosive gases or chemicals
  • Excessive dust or particulates
  • Temperatures below +5°C or above +50°C
  • Vibration or mechanical shock

Storage:

  • Store in climate-controlled environment
  • Avoid extreme temperature cycling
  • Keep sealed in original packaging until deployment
  • Do not store with batteries installed long-term
